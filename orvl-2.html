Why most nail fungus pens disappoint

The UK has dozens of pens on the shelves — in our testing, the large majority failed for the same four reasons.

  • ✕
    Filler-heavy formulas. The label lists botanicals — but the active ingredients are barely there, too diluted to do much for the look of your nails.
  • ✕
    Label ≠ contents. More than once, what was inside didn't match what the front of the pen promised.
  • ✕
    A cheap brush. A wide, sloppy tip drips and wastes the solution instead of placing it precisely on the nail.
  • ✕
    One single ingredient. Instead of a considered blend, many rely on a single oil — and hope the marketing does the rest.

Of every pen we tested, only three delivered what they promised. Those are the three above — and at the very top sits the only one that impressed in every category. If you've already tried a pharmacy brand like Scholl, Nailner or Excilor and been disappointed, this is why: single-ingredient formulas at premium prices, with no precision applicator to get the solution where it needs to go.
